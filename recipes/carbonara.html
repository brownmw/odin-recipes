Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century. A decadent meal that will prove a crowdpleaser. Serves 2.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 100g pancetta
  • 180g spaghetti (or linguine)
  • 70g pecorino cheese
  • black pepper
  • salt

Instructions

  1. Add a small amount of salt to a saucepan (just large enough for the spaghetti) of boiling water, then add the spaghetti and cook until al dente.
  2. Put a frying pan over a moderate to high heat then add the pancetta, cook until crispy.
  3. Crack 2 whole eggs and 1 egg yolk in to a bowl and briefly mix.
  4. Grate the pecorino and mix in with the eggs then season the mixture with a generous amount of black pepper.
  5. Once the pancetta and spaghetti are cooked, using tongs add the spaghetti to the frying pan; don't worry about draining water from the spaghetti.
  6. After spaghetti has been added, also add a ladel or two of the water the pasta cooked in and frying until the water is mostly evaporated.
  7. Take the pan off the heat and allow to cool slightly before pouring in the egg and cheese mix. Keep the pasta and sauce moving continuously to avoid scrambling the eggs, while the cheese melts.
  8. The pasta is now ready to serve, however, you could also move the pan breifly back on to the heat (keeping the pasta moving) in order to thicken the sauce to your preference.
